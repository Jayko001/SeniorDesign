My contribution to this project focused on designing the pipeline that allows the LLM to both understand the data and interact effectively with the user. I also worked on creating and curating the data used in our demo. Compared to the skills I identified last fall, this project built on them in some areas while also pushing me into new ones. For example, my background in AI agents and foundational AI research helped me design a more effective and modular pipeline. However, those same skills were less directly applicable when it came to sourcing and shaping datasets for a non-technical audience, which required a different kind of thinking.
When designing the pipeline, I spent a significant amount of time reading research papers and studying how large agentic systems are structured. This helped me better understand how to manage context, break tasks into steps, and guide the model toward producing more reliable outputs. On the data side, I focused on finding datasets that were both meaningful and accessible to non-technical users, and I experimented with crafting questions that would highlight the strengths of our system. Through this process, I developed stronger skills in system design, prompt structuring, and thinking about user experience. One of my main successes was helping create a pipeline that could dynamically generate and execute data workflows based on user input. At the same time, I ran into challenges around making the system consistent and ensuring the model interpreted data correctly, especially when context was limited or ambiguous. Overall, this project helped me build a better understanding of how to bridge technical AI systems with real user needs.
